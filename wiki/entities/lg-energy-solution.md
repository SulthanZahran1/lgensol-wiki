# LG Energy Solution

## Overview
LG Energy Solution (LGES) is a global leader in lithium-ion battery manufacturing, spun off from LG Chem in 2020. The company is the second-largest EV battery manufacturer worldwide behind CATL, supplying most major global automakers. LGES traces its battery roots to 1995, accumulating over three decades of expertise across cathode, anode, electrolyte, and separator domains. The company produces batteries for electric vehicles (EVs), energy storage systems (ESS), IT devices, and emerging mobility applications. Its headquarters are in Seoul, South Korea, with manufacturing plants across Asia, North America, and Europe spanning joint ventures with General Motors, Stellantis, Honda, and Hyundai. LGES has built a differentiated position through a multi-chemistry portfolio (NCMA, mid-nickel NCM, LFP) and a "one-team" approach that integrates battery cell expertise with system-level software capabilities, most notably through its EMO (Energy Market Optimizer) platform for ESS optimization.

## Technical Details

### ESS Battery Technology
LGES supplies LFP pouch cells for ESS that achieve up to 12% higher energy density versus conventional LFP cells, enabled by lamination and stacking processes. The cells deliver a cycle life of up to 15,000 charge-discharge cycles and incorporate Safety Reinforced Separator (SRS) technology to mitigate internal short circuits. LGES applies Cell-to-Pack (CTP) architecture that simplifies system structure while improving energy density and thermal management. In 2026, LGES announced that its Michigan Lansing plant will produce both pouch-type and prismatic LFP cells, with the latter entering production in 2027 for customers including Tesla's Megapack 3. The company's ESS batteries are manufactured across five North American sites: Holland (MI), Lansing (MI), Ultium Cells Tennessee (joint venture with GM), NextStar Energy Ontario (formerly with Stellantis, now LGES standalone), and the Honda joint venture in Ohio. These hybrid facilities can flexibly switch between EV and ESS production, targeting over 50 GWh of ESS capacity in North America by end of 2026.

### EMO (Energy Market Optimizer) Software
LGES developed EMO as a real-time optimization solution for ESS assets. EMO calculates Dynamic Marketable Energy (DME) — the amount of energy that can actually be sold into the power market — by fusing battery state data (voltage, current, temperature, resistance, State of Charge) with system environmental data and customer operating constraints. The algorithm includes four core modules: DME estimation (targeting 3.5% error for LFP cells in 2026), degradation prediction, anomaly detection/predictive maintenance, and imbalance analysis across cells and racks. EMO is integrated into the AEROS software platform (inherited from the Vertech subsidiary, acquired from NEC Energy Solutions in 2022) and developed jointly by LGES headquarters and Vertech in a "one-team" structure. The roadmap targets 2026 completion of LFP-optimized algorithms and power trading solutions, with field validation in 2027. Flexible warranty terms based on real-time degradation rather than fixed usage patterns are under development.

### Next-Generation Battery Research
LGES's Future Technology Center pursues multiple beyond-lithium-ion chemistries: lithium-sulfur (pouch cells for aviation/UAM), lithium-metal (in partnership with KAIST), all-solid-state (sulfide, oxide, and polymer electrolyte routes, including UCSD collaboration on room-temperature operation), sodium-ion (1st generation for UPS/backup in 2027, 2nd generation targeting 450 Wh/L for EVs), and lithium-manganese-rich (LMR) prismatic cells with GM.

## Significance and LG Context
LGES's strategy reflects the transition from a pure battery component supplier to a full-stack energy solution provider. The company leverages its manufacturing scale — over 90 GWh of new orders targeted for 2026 — and deep cell characterization data (available only to a battery maker) to create software that predicts and optimizes asset value. The five-site North American network, built through joint ventures with [[gm|General Motors]], [[honda|Honda]], and [[stellantis|Stellantis]], now produces both EV and ESS batteries under one roof, providing flexibility as market demand shifts. The Tesla Megapack 3 supply agreement and partnerships with Terra-Gen (8 GWh), Delta Electronics (4 GWh residential), and PGE Polska (1 GWh Zarnowiec) demonstrate LGES's ability to serve large-scale utility, commercial, and residential ESS segments. By integrating EMO into its AEROS platform, LGES aims to become a key enabler of renewable energy integration and grid stability, addressing the core challenge of intermittent renewables highlighted in its own ESS market analysis.
