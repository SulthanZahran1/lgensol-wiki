# Hyundai Motor Group

## Overview
Hyundai Motor Group (HMG) — encompassing Hyundai Motor Company, Kia Corporation, and Genesis — is a foundational strategic partner of LG Energy Solution (LGES). Their collaboration spans over a decade, beginning with the first hybrid battery pack for the 2009 Avante LPi Hybrid and evolving into a multi‑continental manufacturing and technology alliance. Today, the partnership includes two joint‑venture battery cell plants (one in Indonesia, one in the United States), collaborative R&D on next‑generation cell chemistry, and innovative battery‑lifecycle services. HMG’s dedicated electric‑vehicle platform, E-GMP (Electric-Global Modular Platform), is designed around LGES’s high‑nickel NCMA pouch cells, enabling 800‑V ultra‑fast charging. The relationship positions both companies to meet rapidly growing EV demand, comply with regional content regulations, and secure critical mineral supply chains. By 2030, HMG aims to sell 3.6 million EVs annually, a target that underpins LGES’s capacity expansion plans and reinforces their mutual commitment to electrification.

## Technical Details
### Cell Chemistry and Manufacturing
LGES supplies Hyundai and Kia with **NCMA (nickel‑cobalt‑manganese‑aluminum)** lithium‑ion pouch cells. The addition of aluminum stabilizes the cathode structure, allowing a nickel content above 80% while reducing cobalt to less than 10%. This chemistry delivers energy density exceeding 600 Wh/L at the cell level – sufficient to give the Ioniq 6 an EPA range of 361 miles (581 km). Cells are produced in two joint‑venture facilities, each optimized for local market requirements:

- **Indonesia Battery Hub (Karawang, West Java):** 10 GWh annual capacity, operational since 2024. The plant uses locally sourced nickel from Indonesia’s vast laterite deposits, reducing raw‑material logistics costs by up to 30% compared to imports. Cells are assembled into battery systems by Hyundai Mobis and integrated into Hyundai and Kia EVs produced in Asia. The facility is designed for future expansion to 30 GWh, contingent on nickel refining capacity growth in the Indonesia Battery Value Chain.

- **North American Joint Venture (Bryan County, Georgia):** 30 GWh capacity, slated to start production by end‑2025. Total investment exceeds 5.7 trillion KRW (~$4.3 billion), with 50:50 ownership between LGES and HMG. This plant supplies battery cells to HMG’s Metaplant America (HMGMA) and other US assembly lines, ensuring compliance with Inflation Reduction Act (IRA) domestic‑content requirements for EV tax credits. The cells will be used in models such as the Hyundai Ioniq 5/6/9, Kia EV6/EV9, and Genesis GV60/GV70 Electrified. The factory also incorporates advanced dry‑electrode pilot lines, a technology LGES is scaling to reduce solvent use and lower production costs by an estimated 15–20%.

### Ultra‑Fast Charging and System Integration
The E‑GMP platform’s 800‑V architecture, paired with LGES cells, enables a 10–80% charge in approximately 18 minutes using 350‑kW DC fast chargers. Key to this performance is LGES’s proprietary electrolyte formulation that exhibits low viscosity at high temperatures, coupled with a multi‑layer porous anode design that minimizes lithium plating. The battery pack integrates a sophisticated **Battery Management System (BMS)** that monitors individual cell voltage, temperature, and state of charge at 10‑ms intervals. During fast charging, the BMS actively balances temperature across all 384 cells (in a typical Ioniq 5 pack) using a liquid‑cooled cold plate system that maintains cell temperature within 1 °C of target. Safety is further enhanced by a ceramic‑coated separator that prevents dendrite penetration — a feature validated at the cell level with over 1,000 cycles of fast charging without significant capacity fade.

### Battery Services and Lifecycle Management
In a pioneering **Battery‑as‑a‑Service (BaaS)** initiative, LGES and Hyundai Capital launched the “Battery Lifecare” product in late 2023. This lease/rental program uses LGES’s cloud‑based diagnostic platform to assess battery health in real time. Key mechanisms:

- **Residual Value Calculation:** Instead of traditional mileage‑based depreciation, the lease end‑value is derived from the “used battery” condition — capacity fade, internal resistance, and charge history. This lowers monthly payments by approximately 30,000 KRW per month (108,000 KRW over 36 months) for an Ioniq 6. The algorithm uses a neural network trained on over 10 million cell cycles to predict degradation with ±2% accuracy.

- **Reward Score:** Customers earn a “My Reward Score” based on driving habits, charging patterns, and cumulative mileage. Those achieving a target score at lease termination receive a 300,000 KRW incentive — roughly 8% of a typical lease residual value.

- **Self‑Diagnosis:** The vehicle’s BMS communicates with LGES’s servers, giving owners access to battery health data via the Hyundai Capital app (personal) or AutoCare (corporate). This service builds on LGES’s B‑Lifecare consumer app, which won a 2023 National Service Award for innovation in EV battery management.

### Advanced Thermal Management and Safety
LGES has developed a unique **Direct Cooling** design for HMG’s prismatic‑style pouch packs. A dielectric coolant flows through channels embedded in aluminum heat sinks between each cell, removing heat at rates up to 1.5 kW per module. This system maintains cell temperature below 45 °C even during repeated 350‑kW charging sessions, a critical requirement for HMG’s high‑mileage fleet operations. In addition, the pack incorporates a pressure‑release valve and flame‑retardant insulating layers to vent hot gases safely in the event of a thermal runaway — meeting UN R100 and Chinese GB/T 31485 standards. LGES and HMG jointly conducted over 200 abuse tests, including nail penetration, overcharge, and crush, achieving zero propagation in the final pack design.

### Technology Collaboration and Consumer Engagement
Beyond manufacturing, LGES and HMG jointly conducted a “Carbon Zero Camp” in October 2023 at a campsite in Pocheon, South Korea. Participants drove the Ioniq 6 (powered by LGES cells) to the site and used Vehicle‑to‑Load (V2L) functionality to power cooking, lighting, and a mobile cinema — demonstrating zero‑emission energy independence. The event also included a battery education session led by LGES engineers. Such activities align with the companies’ shared goal of promoting EV adoption and carbon neutrality. In 2025, the partnership expanded to develop second‑life energy storage systems using retired EV batteries, targeting commercial buildings and grid‑balancing markets in Korea and the US.

## Significance from LG Energy Solution’s Perspective
The HMG partnership is central to LGES’s global strategy for several reasons:

- **Scale and Revenue:** The two JVs represent a combined ~40 GWh of annual production capacity by 2026, securing a multi‑year, high‑volume customer. HMG’s aggressive EV targets guarantee long‑term demand and provide LGES with stable order books that justify further investment in gigafactories.

- **Supply Chain Resilience:** The Indonesian JV secures access to nickel, a critical cathode material, at a time when global nickel supply is tightening. Indonesia holds the world’s largest nickel reserves (~22 million metric tons), and the plant is integrated with local refining, reducing dependence on Chinese processing. The US JV similarly mitigates tariff exposure and satisfies IRA “foreign entity of concern” rules by using North American‑assembled cells and domestically sourced lithium hydroxide.

- **Product Innovation:** LGES benefits from co‑developing cells optimized for HMG’s platforms — including enhanced fast‑charging capability and advanced safety features — which can later be adapted for other OEMs like [[honda]] and [[stellantis]]. The BaaS initiative creates a new recurring revenue stream from battery diagnostics and leasing, a market projected to grow to $30 billion by 2030.

- **Market Positioning:** By partnering with Korea’s largest automotive group, LGES reinforces its reputation as a global top‑tier battery supplier. The collaboration demonstrates the ability to manage multi‑continent, multi‑OEM relationships — a key differentiator against competitors like CATL and BYD. HMG’s Genesis luxury brand also positions LGES cells at the premium end, commanding higher margins compared to commodity‑grade cells.
