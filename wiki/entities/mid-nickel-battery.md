# High-Voltage Mid-Nickel Battery

## Snapshot
High-voltage mid-nickel batteries use a moderate nickel content and higher operating voltage to balance cost, range, and safety. In this wiki, it is cross-referenced with [[lfp-battery|LFP (Lithium Iron Phosphate) Battery]], [[lmr-battery|Lithium Manganese Rich (LMR) Battery]], and [[ncm-battery|NCM (Ternary) Battery]].

## What To Remember
- Mid-nickel reduces exposure to high nickel cost and stability penalties while retaining better energy density than low-nickel options.
- High-voltage operation needs electrolyte additives, surface coating, and cathode design to suppress side reactions.
- LG Energy Solution presents this as a standard-segment EV option beside high-nickel NCMA and LFP.
