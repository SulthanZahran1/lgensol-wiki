# AVEL

## Overview

AVEL – an acronym for **Add Value to Energy Label** – is an in-house venture (Company-in-Company, CIC) of [[lg-energy-solution|LG Energy Solution]], launched in October 2022. Its core mission is to integrate distributed renewable energy generators (solar, wind) with [[energy-storage-system|ESS (Energy Storage System)]] to form a virtual power plant (VPP). By aggregating generation and storage assets, AVEL forecasts renewable output, optimizes real-time dispatch, and participates in Korea’s wholesale electricity markets as a licensed power broker.

Starting with seven members, AVEL grew to fourteen by early 2024 and operates with the autonomy of an independent business unit. This structure enables rapid decision-making and direct market responsiveness, allowing AVEL to act as a first mover in Korea’s nascent VPP sector. The venture is led by CEO Kim Hyun-tae, who previously drove LG Energy Solution’s energy aggregation task force.

## Technical Details: Energy Aggregation and VPP Platform

AVEL’s technology stack is built around three tightly integrated layers that together enable reliable VPP operations:

1. **Renewable Generation Forecasting** – AVEL employs a hybrid forecasting model that fuses domestic and international meteorological data (from Europe, the US, Japan, and others). In June 2023, the team submitted its first registration test for the Korea Power Exchange (KPX) renewable forecasting system, achieving an error rate of 10.075% – narrowly missing the 10% threshold. Undeterred, AVEL analyzed error sources by resource type and geographic zone, refined its portfolio composition, and in August 2023 achieved a 6.23% error rate on a combined solar‑wind portfolio, earning official registration as a licensed forecasting entity.

2. **Real‑Time Dispatch and Optimization** – The VPP platform continuously monitors grid conditions and ESS state-of-charge (SoC). Its optimization engine computes optimal charge/discharge schedules for each battery asset, maximising revenue from energy arbitrage (buying low, selling high) and ancillary services such as frequency regulation. Dispatch commands are transmitted via secure APIs to KPX market systems within seconds, ensuring that aggregated assets respond to price signals and grid imbalances in near real time.

3. **Market Participation** – AVEL has qualified for two key KPX programmes: the **Renewable Energy Generation Forecasting System** and the **Renewable Energy Real‑Time Bidding Pilot**. These allow AVEL to bid aggregated renewable‑plus‑storage capacity into the wholesale market, effectively operating as a power broker that manages both generation and storage assets. AVEL is currently the only Korean power broker that directly integrates ESS into its forecasting and dispatch operations, a differentiation rooted in LG Energy Solution’s deep battery expertise.

## Jeju Island Pilot Projects

Jeju Island serves as AVEL’s primary testbed because of its high renewable penetration (wind and solar) and island-grid constraints. Two key initiatives have been launched:

- **November 2022** – AVEL signed a business agreement (MOU) with **Jeju Energy Corporation** to jointly develop ESS grid‑integration solutions and stabilise the Jeju power network. The partnership supports Jeju’s “2030 Carbon Zero Island” target, which aims to transition the island to 100% renewable energy.

- **March 2023** – AVEL partnered with **Korea South‑East Power Company (KOEN)** and **Tamra Offshore Wind Power** for a Jeju power market pilot project. This real‑world trial demonstrates how ESS can participate in wholesale markets, providing frequency regulation and peak shaving.

These projects generate critical operational data on ESS cycling under island‑climate conditions, forecasting accuracy in complex terrain, and market settlement mechanisms. AVEL uses this data to refine its algorithms and to propose regulatory improvements that would make ESS installation more affordable for other power brokers – a step toward scaling the VPP model nationwide.

## Significance and LG Context

AVEL is a strategic vehicle for [[lg-energy-solution|LG Energy Solution]] to move beyond battery cell manufacturing into **Energy as a Service (EaaS)**. By leveraging LGES’s proprietary knowledge of cell chemistry, BMS (Battery Management System) design, and large‑scale ESS deployment, AVEL optimises battery life and safety within VPP operations – a capability few competitors can match.

AVEL’s business model creates recurring revenue streams from three sources: grid service fees, forecasting subscriptions, and ESS operation contracts. The venture aims to capture **over 30% of the domestic VPP market by 2025–2026**, then expand to mainland Korea and eventually to North America and Europe, where VPP and energy aggregation markets are more mature.

Beyond technology, AVEL actively works on **regulatory improvements** – for example, advocating for incentive structures that reduce upfront ESS costs for other brokers. This dual focus on software and policy positions AVEL as a key enabler of Korea’s renewable energy transition.
