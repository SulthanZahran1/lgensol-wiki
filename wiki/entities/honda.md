# Honda

## Overview

Honda Motor Co., Ltd. is a Japanese multinational automaker and motorcycle manufacturer that entered into a landmark joint venture with LG Energy Solution (LGES) in 2022 to produce lithium-ion batteries for North American electric vehicles (EVs). This partnership represents the first strategic collaboration between a Korean battery manufacturer and a Japanese automaker, breaking long-standing national supply chain boundaries. Beyond passenger EVs, LGES and Honda are also cooperating on electric two-wheeler battery-swapping infrastructure in Southeast Asia, particularly in Vietnam, leveraging Honda's dominant position in the motorcycle market.

## Technical Details

### Ohio Joint Venture Plant

LG Energy Solution and Honda formed a 51:49 joint venture (LGES holding majority) named **L-H Battery Company, Inc.** (provisional name) to build a dedicated battery manufacturing facility in **Fayette County, Ohio**, near Jeffersonville. The site spans approximately **186,000 m²** and required a total investment of **US $4.4 billion** (₩5.1 trillion). Construction began in early 2023, with completion targeted for the end of 2024 and mass production starting in **late 2025**. The inaugural CEO is LGES North America head Lee Hyuk-jae, and the COO is Rick Riggle, formerly of Honda's Anna Engine Plant.

The plant is designed for an annual production capacity of **40 GWh** — enough to supply batteries for approximately **500,000 high-performance pure EVs** per year. It produces **pouch-type lithium-ion cells and modules** specifically engineered for Honda and Acura EV models built on Honda's first-generation dedicated EV platform, the **e:Architecture**. The facility will create an estimated **2,200 direct jobs** and is strategically located within Honda's existing North American manufacturing network in Ohio (Marysville, East Liberty, Anna engine plant), enabling just-in-time delivery and qualifying for Inflation Reduction Act (IRA) domestic content incentives.

Key technical features:
- Pouch cell format (LGES's core technology for passenger EVs)
- Exclusive supply to Honda's North American assembly lines
- State-of-the-art smart factory with automated quality control
- Production of both cells and modules on-site

### Vietnam Battery-Swapping Pilot for Two-Wheelers

In May 2026, LGES, Honda, and the Hanoi city government signed a Memorandum of Understanding (MOU) for a public **Battery Swapping Station (BSS)** network for electric motorcycles and scooters. The pilot will deploy **~50 swapping stations** in central Hanoi and provide **500 electric two-wheelers** (e-motorcycles) initially, starting in Q3 2026.

The batteries used are LGES's **2170 cylindrical cells** (21 mm diameter, 70 mm height) — the same form factor used in Tesla vehicles, but adapted for light mobility. LGES provides the cells, battery management system (BMS) for safety monitoring, and the station operation software. Honda supplies the **Mobile Power Pack (MPP)** — the standardized battery pack — the swapping stations, and the electric motorcycles. Hanoi city government handles permits, grid connections, and operational support.

This initiative is driven by Hanoi's plan to phase out internal combustion engine motorcycles in the city center from July 2026, with a full ban by **2030**. Vietnam has an estimated **80 million motorcycles** (about 4% electric as of 2025), and the electric two-wheeler market is expected to grow at >18% CAGR, making it a critical arena for battery demand.

## Strategic Significance

The LGES-Honda joint venture is historically significant as the first direct battery production collaboration between a Korean cell maker and a Japanese carmaker. Traditionally, Japanese OEMs sourced batteries from domestic suppliers (Panasonic, GS Yuasa) or developed their own. This partnership signals a fundamental shift — Japanese automakers now recognize the need to partner with global leaders in cost-competitive, high-volume cell manufacturing, especially for the North American market under IRA rules.

For LGES, the Honda JV diversifies its customer base beyond Detroit's Big Three and provides access to Honda's reputation for quality and brand trust. For Honda, it secures a dedicated, localized supply of pouch cells optimized for its e:Architecture, avoiding the risks of relying solely on Japanese or Chinese suppliers. The JV also strengthens LGES's North American manufacturing footprint, complementing its existing joint ventures with [[gm|General Motors]] ([[ultium-cells|Ultium Cells]]), [[stellantis]], and its standalone facility in Arizona.

The partnership also extends into emerging markets: the Vietnam BSS pilot represents a blueprint for two-wheeler electrification across Southeast Asia, where Honda commands ~86% of the motorcycle market (VAMM 2025 data). LGES gains operational experience with swapping infrastructure and second-life battery management, while Honda advances its 2050 carbon neutrality target for all products and corporate activities.
