# Ford

## Overview
Ford Motor Company is a cornerstone strategic partner of [[lg-energy-solution|LG Energy Solution]] (LGES), with a collaboration that began in 2011 supplying batteries for the Ford Focus Electric. Over 15 years, the partnership has evolved into a multi-contract, multi-region relationship covering passenger EVs, commercial vehicles, and end-of-life battery recycling. As of 2026, LGES and Ford have signed cumulative battery supply agreements totaling 109 GWh—one of the largest single-OEM commitments in LGES’s portfolio.

The partnership directly supports Ford’s Ford+ electrification strategy, which targets 2 million+ annual EV production by 2026 and 50% EV sales share by 2030. Ford is Europe’s best-selling commercial vehicle brand (8 consecutive years through 2022), with its Transit and E‑Transit models holding the global light commercial vehicle sales lead for five years running. This commercial-vehicle emphasis drives the technical requirements for LGES’s supply: high power output, long cycle life, and durability under extreme conditions.

## Technical Details

### Supply Contract Structure
The 109 GWh total comprises two separate long-term agreements:
- **75 GWh** covering 2027–2032 (six years), reserved for Ford’s next-generation commercial vehicle platforms.
- **34 GWh** covering 2026–2030 (five years), supporting existing models including the Mustang Mach‑E and E‑Transit.

Both contracts were originally envisioned for fulfillment through a proposed joint venture with Koç Holding (Ford Otosan) in Ankara, Turkey—a 25–45 GWh plant. However, due to evolving market conditions, the plan was restructured to leverage LGES’s existing production lines at its Wroclaw, Poland plant. This shift improved time-to-market and capital efficiency while still securing localized supply for Ford’s European commercial vehicle production (Ford Otosan manufactures ~450,000 commercial vehicles annually, mostly for Europe). The Wroclaw plant underwent a **2× capacity expansion** in 2023 specifically for Ford, and continues to ramp.

In 2025, LGES and Ford agreed to migrate Mustang Mach‑E battery production from Poland to LGES’s Michigan plant, enabling compliance with U.S. Inflation Reduction Act (IRA) tax credit requirements for North American assembly. This demonstrates the flexibility of the partnership to adapt to regulatory and market shifts.

### Battery Technology and Commercial Vehicle Focus
LGES supplies **NCM (nickel‑cobalt‑manganese) pouch cells** for Ford’s EVs. For the commercial vehicle segment (E‑Transit, next-gen vans), cells are engineered to meet demanding specifications:
- **High power output** for heavy payloads and frequent stop‑start duty cycles.
- **Extended cycle life** (＞300,000 km or 10+ years of service).
- **Rugged durability** for operation in extreme weather (snow, rain, heat) and rough driving conditions.

These premium batteries command higher margins than passenger‑car cells, and the long‑term contracts provide revenue stability for LGES. The commercial vehicle focus is particularly valuable in Europe, where the electric LCV market is expected to grow at a CAGR of ~36% and reach over 50% EV penetration by 2030.

### Recycling Joint Venture with DBG (France)
As part of the circular economy strategy, LGES and Ford partnered with France’s leading metal recycler Derichebourg (DBG) to establish a battery recycling joint venture in Val d’Oise, north of Paris. The facility, scheduled for construction start in 2026 and commercial operation in 2027, will have an annual processing capacity of **20,000 tonnes** of end‑of‑life batteries and manufacturing scrap.

The process involves:
1. **Collection** via DBG’s network of 200+ collection sites across France.
2. **Shredding and mechanical separation** to produce **black mass**—a powder containing lithium, nickel, cobalt, and manganese.
3. **Downstream hydrometallurgical refining** (by third parties or LGES affiliates) to recover critical metals, which are then fed into LGES’s cathode production, closing the loop.

France accounted for 15% of European EV sales in 2024, ensuring a growing stream of end‑of‑life batteries. The venture also helps both companies comply with the EU Battery Regulation, which mandates minimum recycled content: 16% cobalt and 6% lithium by 2031, rising to 26% and 12% respectively by 2036.

## Significance / LG Context
The Ford partnership is a linchpin of LGES’s European operations. The Wroclaw plant—LGES’s largest European manufacturing hub—depends on Ford contracts for a substantial share of its capacity. The commercial vehicle segment is especially attractive: premium performance requirements yield higher margins, and long‑term contracts provide multi‑year revenue visibility.

The restructuring of the Turkey JV to a supply‑only arrangement illustrates LGES’s operational agility. Instead of committing capital to a new greenfield plant, LGES utilized existing capacity, demonstrating a customer‑first approach that balances scale with capital efficiency.

The recycling JV with DBG marks LGES’s first battery recycling venture in Europe, reinforcing a commitment to end‑to‑end lifecycle management. It complements LGES’s global recycling network (North America, Korea) and positions the company to meet tightening EU recycled‑content mandates. For Ford, the JV secures a domestic source of recycled materials for future battery production, aligning with its sustainability goals.

Beyond numbers, the partnership is a showcase for LGES pouch cell technology. The Mustang Mach‑E—named Consumer Reports’ top EV in 2022—was displayed at InterBattery 2023 with LGES branding, underscoring the technological endorsement.
