## Concepts

- [[active-material|Active Material]] - Active material is the electrochemically active powder in an electrode that stores and releases lithium ions.
- [[anode-materials|Anode Material]] - Anode materials host lithium during charging and release it during discharge.
- [[assembly-process|Assembly Process]] - The assembly process turns finished electrodes and separator into a physical cell stack, jelly roll, or pouch structure.
- [[battery-management-system|BMS (Battery Management System)]] - A BMS monitors and controls battery operation so cells stay inside safe voltage, current, temperature, and health limits.
- [[battery-manufacturing|Battery Manufacturing Process Overview]] - Battery manufacturing converts powders, foils, separator, electrolyte, and cases into tested cells and packs.
- [[battery-recycling|End-of-Life Battery Reuse and Recycling]] - Battery recycling recovers valuable metals and materials from production scrap and end-of-life batteries.
- [[battery-safety|Battery Safety and Thermal Runaway]] - Battery safety is the combined control of electrochemistry, materials, design, manufacturing, BMS, and pack-level protection.
- [[binder|Binder]] - Binder holds active material and conductive additive together and anchors the coating to the current collector.
- [[bmts|BMTS (B.around Total Solution)]] - BMTS is LG Energy Solution's broader battery-management total solution, extending BMS into diagnosis, service, and life-cycle data.
- [[cathode-materials|Cathode Material]] - Cathode materials determine much of a lithium-ion cell's voltage, capacity, cost, and thermal behavior.
- [[cell-balancing|Cell Balancing]] - Cell balancing reduces imbalance among series-connected cells so usable pack capacity and safety are not limited by the weakest cell.
- [[cell-to-pack|Cell-To-Pack (CTP)]] - Cell-to-pack removes or reduces module-level structure so cells integrate more directly into the battery pack.
- [[conductive-additive|Conductive Additive (CNT)]] - Conductive additive creates electron pathways through an electrode coating whose active material may be poorly conductive.
- [[critical-minerals|Battery Critical Mineral Supply Chain]] - Critical minerals are the lithium, nickel, cobalt, manganese, graphite, copper, aluminum, and related inputs that shape battery cost and resilience.
- [[current-collector|Current Collector]] - Current collectors are metal foils that collect electrons from electrode coatings and connect them to tabs or terminals.
- [[dry-electrode-process|Dry Electrode Process]] - Dry electrode processing makes electrodes without the solvent-heavy wet slurry route.
- [[electrode-process|Electrode Process]] - The electrode process makes coated cathode and anode rolls through mixing, coating, drying, calendering, and slitting/notching.
- [[electrolyte|Electrolyte / Electrolyte Solution]] - Electrolyte transports lithium ions between cathode and anode while electrons travel through the external circuit.
- [[electrolyte-additive|Electrolyte Additive]] - Electrolyte additives are small-formulation ingredients that tune interphases, gas generation, safety, and lifetime.
- [[energy-storage-system|ESS (Energy Storage System)]] - An ESS stores electricity for later use, usually at grid, commercial, data-center, renewable, or home scale.
- [[fast-charging|Fast Charging]] - Fast charging raises charging power while trying to avoid lithium plating, overheating, excessive SEI growth, and accelerated aging.
- [[formation-aging|Activation Process (Formation & Aging)]] - Formation and aging activate a new cell, form stable interphases, and screen early defects before shipment.
- [[humanoid-robot-battery|Batteries for Humanoid Robots]] - Humanoid robot batteries must combine high energy, high power, safety, compact packaging, and long cycle life.
- [[lithium-ion-battery|Lithium-Ion Battery Structure & Operating Principle]] - A lithium-ion battery stores energy by shuttling lithium ions between cathode and anode through electrolyte while electrons flow externally.
- [[pack-process|Pack Process]] - The pack process integrates cells into modules or packs with structure, sensing, thermal control, high-voltage paths, and protection.
- [[pre-dispersion|Pre-Dispersion Process]] - Pre-dispersion prepares hard-to-disperse conductive materials or powders before final slurry mixing.
- [[separator|Separator]] - The separator is a porous membrane that prevents cathode and anode contact while letting ions pass through electrolyte-filled pores.
- [[silicon-anode|Silicon Anode Material]] - Silicon anode material can store far more lithium than graphite but expands strongly during charging.
- [[uam-battery|Batteries for UAM]] - UAM batteries serve urban air mobility aircraft, where weight, power, safety, and reliability dominate design choices.
- [[welding-process|Welding Process]] - Welding connects electrode tabs, current collectors, cans, modules, and busbars into low-resistance electrical paths.

## Glossary

- [[anodeless-battery|Anodeless Battery]] - An anodeless battery starts without a host anode layer; lithium plates onto the anode current collector during charging.
- [[baas|BaaS (Battery as a Service)]] - BaaS means Battery as a Service: battery value sold through diagnostics, leasing, swapping, life management, or data services.
- [[battery-passport|Battery Passport]] - A battery passport is a digital record of a battery's materials, manufacturing, performance, carbon footprint, and life-cycle history.
- [[bbu|BBU (Battery Backup Unit)]] - A BBU, or Battery Backup Unit, supplies backup power when the primary power source fails.
- [[bi-cell|Bi-Cell]] - A bi-cell is a laminated sub-unit used in stacked pouch-cell construction.
- [[c-rate|C-rate (Current Rate / Charge-Discharge Rate)]] - C-rate expresses charge or discharge current relative to nominal capacity.
- [[cc-cv-charging|CC/CV Charging (Constant Current/Constant Voltage Charging)]] - CC/CV charging uses a constant-current phase followed by a constant-voltage phase.
- [[cei|CEI (Cathode Electrolyte Interphase)]] - CEI means Cathode Electrolyte Interphase, the interphase that forms on cathode surfaces.
- [[coin-cell|Coin-Cell]] - A coin cell is a small button-like cell format widely used for material screening and small electronics.
- [[coulombic-efficiency|Coulombic Efficiency (CE)]] - Coulombic efficiency is the ratio of discharge capacity to charge capacity in a cycle.
- [[dendrite|Dendrite]] - A dendrite is a needle-like metal deposit that can grow during uneven lithium plating.
- [[dld|DLD (Double Layer Slot Die Coating)]] - DLD means Double Layer Slot Die coating, a method that coats two layers with slot-die equipment.
- [[dod|DoD (Depth of Discharge)]] - DoD, or Depth of Discharge, indicates how much of a battery's capacity has been used.
- [[eaas|EaaS (Energy as a Service)]] - EaaS means Energy as a Service: customers buy energy outcomes rather than owning every asset directly.
- [[ems|EMS (Energy Management System)]] - EMS means Energy Management System, software that supervises power generation, storage, and load.
- [[gas-free-solvent|Gas Free Solvent]] - Gas-free solvent refers to electrolyte solvent design intended to suppress gas generation during cell operation.
- [[internal-resistance|Internal Resistance]] - Internal resistance is the effective resistance inside a cell that causes voltage drop and heat generation under current.
- [[ionic-conductivity|Ionic Conductivity]] - Ionic conductivity measures how easily ions move through electrolyte or solid electrolyte.
- [[mah|mAh (milliampere-hour)]] - mAh, or milliampere-hour, is a capacity unit equal to current in milliamps multiplied by time in hours.
- [[mono-cell|Mono-Cell]] - A mono-cell is a basic laminated electrode-separator unit in stacked pouch-cell assembly.
- [[pcm|PCM (Protection Circuit Module)]] - PCM means Protection Circuit Module, electronics that protect a battery from overcharge, overdischarge, overcurrent, and short circuits.
- [[pcs|PCS (Power Conversion System)]] - PCS means Power Conversion System, the inverter/converter hardware that moves power between batteries and AC or DC grids.
- [[potential-window|Potential Window]] - The potential window is the voltage range over which an electrolyte or material remains electrochemically stable.
- [[quaternary-battery|Quaternary Battery]] - A quaternary battery commonly refers to a four-element cathode family such as NCMA.
- [[sei|SEI (Solid Electrolyte Interphase)]] - SEI means Solid Electrolyte Interphase, the thin layer formed mainly on the anode during early charging.
- [[soc|SoC (State of Charge)]] - SoC means State of Charge, the estimated remaining charge as a percentage of usable capacity.
- [[soh|SoH (State of Health)]] - SoH means State of Health, an estimate of how much battery capability remains compared with a fresh cell.
- [[sox|SoX (State of X)]] - SoX is a family term for state estimators such as SoC, SoH, state of power, and state of safety.
- [[srs|SRS (Safety Reinforced Separator)]] - SRS is Safety Reinforced Separator, LG Energy Solution's ceramic-coated separator safety technology.
- [[ternary-battery|Ternary Battery]] - A ternary battery uses a three-metal cathode system, usually NCM or NCA-type layered oxides.
- [[tp-prevention|TP Prevention Technology (Thermal Propagation Prevention)]] - TP prevention means thermal propagation prevention: design measures that stop one failing cell from spreading heat to neighbors.
- [[ups|UPS (Uninterruptible Power Supply)]] - UPS means Uninterruptible Power Supply, a system that provides immediate backup power during outages.
