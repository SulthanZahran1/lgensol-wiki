# DLD (Double Layer Slot Die Coating)

## Overview / Introduction

Double Layer Slot Die Coating (DLD) is a proprietary electrode manufacturing technology developed and commercialized by LG Energy Solution. First applied to anode slurries in 2018, DLD represents the world's first commercial implementation of dual-layer coating for lithium-ion battery electrodes. The technology enables two distinct electrode slurries to be simultaneously deposited onto a current collector in a single pass, forming a functionally graded electrode with optimized performance. DLD directly addresses one of the most critical customer demands: fast charging. With DLD, electric vehicles can charge from 10% to 80% state of charge in under 20 minutes—roughly the time needed to listen to six or seven songs. This breakthrough has reshaped consumer expectations for EV convenience and has been a key enabler of LG Energy Solution's leadership in the global battery market.

## Technical Details

### The Electrode Coating Process

The electrode manufacturing sequence consists of mixing, coating, drying, calendering, slitting, and notching. During mixing, active material, binder, conductive additive, and solvent are blended into a homogeneous slurry. The slurry is then applied onto a metal foil current collector (copper for anode, aluminum for cathode) using a slot die coating head. In conventional single-layer coating, one homogeneous slurry composition is applied across the entire electrode thickness. DLD modifies the coating step by using a special slot die with two independent flow channels. These channels allow two different slurry formulations to be deposited wet-on-wet as distinct top and bottom layers. The layers are dried together, forming a single integrated electrode coating with graded composition through the thickness. The slot die head is designed to maintain a stable interface between the two layers without intermixing, and drying temperature profiles are optimized to prevent binder migration and ensure uniform layer adhesion.

### Mechanism of Functional Grading

In a DLD electrode, the bottom layer (adjacent to the current collector) can be engineered for strong adhesion and low electrical resistance. The top layer (facing the separator) can be optimized for fast lithium-ion transport and electrochemical stability. This vertical functional grading is impossible with a single homogeneous formulation. For example, the binder content can be concentrated in the bottom layer to enhance mechanical integrity, while the top layer uses a lower binder fraction to maximize ionic conductivity. Alternatively, the active material particle size can be graded: smaller particles in the top layer to shorten diffusion paths, larger particles in the bottom layer to improve packing density and adhesion. This dual-layer architecture allows independent optimization of mechanical, electrical, and electrochemical properties.

### Performance Improvements

By distributing the binder more uniformly and efficiently, DLD reduces internal resistance by approximately 15–20% compared to single-layer coatings. Lower internal resistance directly translates to faster charging rates. In LG Energy Solution's testing, DLD-equipped anodes enabled electric vehicle batteries to reach 80% state of charge in less than 20 minutes. The technology also improves electrode mechanical properties: the dual-layer structure provides better crack resistance during calendering and cycling, extending cycle life by up to 10% under fast-charging conditions. DLD also enhances binder efficiency—the same amount of binder can achieve stronger adhesion and better electrochemical performance, allowing for potential reduction in binder content and cost.

### Production Efficiency

DLD is implemented using roll-to-roll slot die coaters with precision control of two separate slurry feeds. Because the two layers are applied in one pass, production throughput is higher than sequential two-pass coating, reducing capital equipment and floor space requirements. The wet-on-wet deposition avoids the need for intermediate drying, saving energy and time. This makes DLD not only a performance-enhancing technology but also a manufacturing efficiency improvement. LG Energy Solution has successfully integrated DLD into its mass production lines for both automotive pouch cells and small-format cylindrical cells (2170, 1865, and pouch cells for IT devices).

## Significance / LG Context

DLD is considered a platform technology because the dual-layer concept can be adapted to different performance targets—fast charging, high energy density, long cycle life, or combinations thereof. LG Energy Solution has applied DLD across its electrode production lines for vehicles such as the Volkswagen ID.3, Porsche Taycan, and Renault Mégane e-Tech. At InterBattery Europe 2023, LG highlighted that the Porsche Taycan uses DLD combined with silicon anode technology to achieve 100 km of range in just 5 minutes of charging.

DLD is one of LG Energy Solution's most valuable intellectual property assets. The company holds extensive patents covering the dual-layer coating method, slurry compositions, and electrode architectures. In recent legal campaigns, LG has identified more than 580 specific patent infringements by competitors, many involving DLD. The company considers DLD a "strategic patent" that is extremely difficult to design around—leading industry analysts to remark that "it is impossible to manufacture a battery without infringing LG's DLD patents." The technology forms a critical part of LG's IP portfolio, which includes over 32,000 registered patents and 58,000 patent applications globally.

DLD complements other advanced manufacturing techniques in LG's portfolio, such as [[azs-stacking|AZS (Advanced Z-Stacking)]] for cell assembly and the [[dry-electrode-process|Dry Electrode Process]] for solvent-free coating. Together, these technologies form a manufacturing ecosystem that enables higher energy density, faster charging, and lower cost. The continued development of DLD is expected to support future cell formats, including 46-series cylindrical batteries and next-generation anode materials like silicon.
