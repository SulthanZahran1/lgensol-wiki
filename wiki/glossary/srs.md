# SRS (Safety Reinforced Separator)

## Overview / Introduction

The Safety Reinforced Separator (SRS®) is LG Energy Solution’s proprietary ceramic‑coated separator technology, originally developed by LG Chem in 2004. As one of the four essential components of a lithium‑ion battery—alongside the cathode, anode, and electrolyte—the separator is directly responsible for battery safety. Its core function is to physically separate the positive and negative electrodes to prevent internal short circuits while allowing lithium ions to pass through its porous structure. SRS enhances this fundamental role by depositing a thin ceramic‑polymer composite layer onto a conventional polyolefin separator (polyethylene or polypropylene), dramatically improving heat resistance and mechanical robustness.

Over two decades of continuous development, SRS has become a cornerstone of LG Energy Solution’s safety strategy, especially for high‑energy‑density cells used in premium electric vehicles and energy storage systems. The technology is integrated into the company’s stack‑and‑folding assembly process, where it works in concert with [[bi-cell]] units to create a dual‑layer safety barrier.

## Technical Details

**Coating composition and structure.** SRS employs ceramic nanoparticles—typically alumina (Al₂O₃) or boehmite (AlOOH)—dispersed in a polymer binder. This slurry is coated onto one or both sides of the separator substrate, achieving a total coating thickness of a few micrometers. The ceramic particles form a rigid, highly thermally stable layer while the binder ensures adhesion and flexibility during cell assembly.

**Thermal stability.** Conventional polyolefin separators begin to shrink and lose mechanical integrity above 100 °C, which can lead to electrode contact and thermal runaway. SRS‑coated separators withstand temperatures up to 200 °C without significant shrinkage or deformation. This high‑temperature resilience is critical for preventing internal shorts during abusive conditions such as overcharging, external heating, or nail penetration. In LG Energy Solution’s own tests, SRS maintains dimensional stability even under prolonged exposure to elevated temperatures, providing a wider safety margin than uncoated separators.

**Mechanical strength and dendrite resistance.** The ceramic coating adds puncture and tear resistance. In high‑energy‑density cells, lithium dendrites can grow during fast charging and pierce a conventional separator, causing a short. The hard ceramic particles physically block dendrite penetration, thereby improving cycle life and safety. The coating also improves the separator’s compressive strength, which is beneficial during the calendering (pressing) step in electrode manufacturing and under the stack pressure inside a completed cell.

**Electrolyte wettability.** The hydrophilic nature of ceramic oxides enhances the separator’s ability to absorb and retain liquid electrolyte. Improved wetting increases ionic conductivity and ensures uniform lithium‑ion transport across the electrode area, which can boost rate capability and overall cell performance. This is especially important for thick, high‑loading electrodes used in high‑energy cells, where electrolyte penetration must be fast and homogeneous.

**Process integration and dual‑layer safety.** In LG Energy Solution’s stack‑and‑folding process, SRS can be fabricated as a standalone coated separator or as a separate binder‑polymer‑active‑layer film that is laminated with the electrode assembly. When used between [[bi-cell]] units—each unit having a ‘cathode/separator/anode/separator/cathode’ or analogous arrangement—the SRS film forms an external safety layer. This results in a double layer of protection: the internal separators within each bi‑cell and the external SRS film between them. This architecture further minimizes the risk of short circuits, especially in high‑energy pouch cells where the electrode stack is large and subject to mechanical stress.

## Significance / LG Context

SRS is particularly vital for high‑nickel battery chemistries such as the [[ncma-battery|NCMA (Quaternary) Battery]] and [[high-nickel-battery|High‑Nickel Battery]], which operate at higher voltages and generate more heat than conventional cells. For example, the NCMA chemistry used in LG’s Premium electric‑vehicle segment demands separators that can maintain structural integrity under elevated temperatures without sacrificing energy density. SRS enables these cells to achieve longer driving ranges and faster charging while satisfying rigorous safety standards.

Over the past two decades, LG Energy Solution has continuously refined SRS. The company has integrated the technology into its pouch‑cell manufacturing lines alongside stacking techniques like [[bi-cell]] and [[mono-cell|Mono‑Cell]]. SRS is often paired with other safety innovations such as [[gas-free-solvent|Gas Free Solvent]] and [[tp-prevention|TP Prevention Technology]] to create a multi‑layered safety system. This holistic approach has been a key differentiator in LG’s battery products, especially for electric vehicles and energy storage systems where thermal runaway prevention is paramount.

By allowing the use of thinner, more energy‑dense electrodes without compromising mechanical robustness, SRS directly supports LG’s roadmap toward higher energy‑density cells—including those with nickel content above 90% and silicon‑anode technologies. The technology also contributes to improved cycle life by reducing the incidence of separator‑related failures over thousands of charge‑discharge cycles.
