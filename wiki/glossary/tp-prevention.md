# TP Prevention Technology (Thermal Propagation Prevention)

## Overview

TP (Thermal Propagation) prevention technology is a pack‑level safety strategy designed to stop heat generated by a single failing battery cell from spreading to neighboring cells. It acts as the second line of defense after thermal runaway (TR) prevention at the cell level. As lithium‑ion batteries increase in energy density, more cells are packed into confined spaces, raising the risk of accumulated heat and cascading failure. TP prevention addresses this by combining thermal barriers, venting systems, active cooling, and advanced detection to contain any thermal event within a single cell, ensuring the overall safety of the battery pack. In applications such as electric vehicles (EVs), light electric vehicles (LEVs), and urban air mobility (UAM), TP prevention is increasingly mandated by safety regulations.

## Technical Details

### Mechanisms of Thermal Propagation

Thermal propagation begins when a single cell enters thermal runaway. TR is triggered when internal temperature exceeds approximately 130 °C, initiating exothermic reactions—decomposition of the solid electrolyte interphase (SEI), electrolyte breakdown, and cathode oxygen release. These reactions generate additional heat in a self‑accelerating cycle, raising temperatures above 800 °C. Without intervention, intense heat transfers to adjacent cells through conduction, convection, and radiation, potentially causing a domino effect of failures throughout the pack. The hot gases and particles produced during TR further accelerate propagation by igniting nearby cells.

### Passive Prevention: Thermal Barriers and Materials

Multiple passive materials block heat transfer between cells. Ceramic pads, silicone sheets, and aerogel blankets are placed as thermal barriers to reduce conduction. Phase‑change materials (PCMs) absorb large amounts of latent heat as they melt, slowing temperature rise. Air gaps between cells reduce radiative heat transfer, while increased spacing adds thermal resistance. In addition, flame‑retardant additives mixed into the electrolyte suppress combustion and delay ignition of adjacent cells. These passive measures provide the first line of defense at the pack level, often combined with cell clustering—dividing the pack into isolated physical zones to prevent propagation from one zone to another.

### Active Prevention: Venting and Cooling

To manage the by‑products of TR, TP systems incorporate venting channels that direct hot gases and particles away from neighboring cells. This reduces the concentration of flammable species and lowers the risk of ignition. Active cooling systems—using either forced air or liquid flow—distribute heat away from hot spots, maintaining safe temperature gradients across the pack. In some designs, the BMS actively adjusts coolant flow upon detecting abnormal temperature rise, providing an additional layer of containment.

### Proactive Detection Technologies

Early detection is critical to prevent TP. LG Energy Solution employs several diagnostic tools that monitor cell health in real time.

- **Lithium plating diagnostic**: Uses pulse signals and electrochemical impedance spectroscopy (EIS) analysis to detect early signs of lithium deposition—a precursor to internal short circuits and thermal runaway.
- **BMIC‑integrated EIS**: The Battery Management IC continuously monitors impedance changes, predicting temperature anomalies before they escalate.
- **Gas and pressure sensors**: Directly detect internal cell reactions (e.g., venting gases) well before a full thermal event occurs.

This preemptive approach shifts safety from reactive containment to early warning and prevention, enabling the BMS to take corrective actions (e.g., reducing charge current or activating cooling) before propagation can begin.

## Significance and LG Context

TP prevention is a mandatory safety requirement for EVs, large‑scale ESS, and emerging applications like UAM. Regulatory frameworks increasingly demand that a battery pack prevent propagation for a minimum period (e.g., 5 minutes) after a single cell enters thermal runaway—giving occupants time to evacuate. In the UAM market, where failure in mid‑air has catastrophic consequences, TP prevention is the cornerstone of certification by authorities such as the FAA, EASA, and CAAC.

LG Energy Solution’s comprehensive TP strategy integrates multiple layers of defense:

- **Cell‑level**: [[SRS]] (Safety Reinforced Separator) technology coats the separator with ceramic particles and a polymer binder, enabling it to withstand temperatures up to 200 °C (compared to conventional separators that degrade above 100 °C). This prevents internal short circuits and delays TR onset.
- **Material innovation**: [[gas-free-solvent]] reduces gas generation during abuse, lowering pressure and thermal load.
- **Pack‑level design**: LG engineers develop modular pack concepts incorporating thermal barriers, venting channels, and cell clustering tailored to specific applications—from LEV (E‑bikes and scooters) to high‑energy EV packs. For example, the E‑bike pack development team has already created a TP prevention solution that meets anticipated international standards, giving LG a competitive edge as regulations evolve.
- **Smart diagnostics**: The BMS integrates lithium plating detection, real‑time EIS, and sensor fusion to predict and prevent thermal events. LG’s modular pack designers use simulation and iterative testing to optimize the balance between energy density and safety.

This multi‑layered architecture positions LG Energy Solution as a leader in battery safety, meeting the most stringent global standards and enabling high‑energy‑density packs without compromising safety. The company’s proactive investment in TP technology not only complies with existing regulations but also anticipates future requirements, ensuring that its products remain at the forefront of the industry.
