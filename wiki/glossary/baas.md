# BaaS (Battery as a Service)

## Overview / Introduction

Battery as a Service (BaaS) is a business model that shifts the battery industry from one-time cell sales to service-centric value delivery. Instead of purchasing a battery outright, users access battery capabilities through leasing, swapping, diagnostics, lifecycle management, and data analytics. BaaS evolved from the broader Energy as a Service (EaaS) concept, where energy infrastructure is managed as a service. As battery costs remain a significant barrier to electric vehicle (EV) adoption and regulatory demands for circularity intensify, BaaS has emerged as a critical enabler of sustainable mobility and energy storage.

In a BaaS model, the battery is treated as a managed asset throughout its entire lifecycle—from production through use, second-life applications in [[energy-storage-system|Energy Storage Systems (ESS)]], and final recycling. The approach is particularly relevant as the European Union mandates digital Battery Passports from 2027, requiring full traceability of battery materials, state of health, and end-of-life handling.

## Technical Details

BaaS encompasses several distinct service models, each enabled by cloud platforms, AI algorithms, secure data transmission, and standardized battery pack designs.

**Battery Swapping** allows users to exchange depleted batteries for fully charged units at dedicated swapping stations. This eliminates charging wait times, reducing downtime to approximately 20 seconds per swap—far faster than even ultra-fast DC charging (typically 30+ minutes to 80% capacity). Swapping is especially suited to two-wheelers, three-wheelers, and fleet vehicles where rapid turnaround is critical. At each exchange, the battery is inspected, and its state of health (SoH) is updated in the cloud, enabling proactive maintenance and lifecycle tracking.

**Battery Monitoring and Diagnostics** uses cloud-connected Battery Management Systems (BMS) to collect real-time data on voltage, temperature, state of charge (SoC), and SoH. Machine learning models analyze these data to predict remaining useful life, detect overcharge/overdischarge risks, and recommend optimal charging profiles. Users can view battery health via smartphone apps, and the accumulated history enhances trust in used-vehicle transactions.

**Battery Subscription and Leasing** separates battery cost from vehicle cost, lowering the upfront purchase price of EVs. The service provider retains ownership, managing performance degradation risks and guaranteeing a minimum capacity. This model also facilitates end-of-life collection and recycling, as the provider controls the asset.

**Full Lifecycle Management** integrates all the above, managing batteries from cradle to grave. After first life in an EV (typically 8–10 years), batteries with residual capacity are repurposed in stationary ESS. When no longer suitable, critical minerals such as nickel, cobalt, lithium, manganese, and graphite are recovered through hydrometallurgical or pyrometallurgical processes and returned to the manufacturing loop—closing the resource cycle. A unified cloud platform tracks every stage, enabling real-time asset optimization.

## Significance and LG Energy Solution Context

BaaS offers multiple benefits: it reduces upfront EV costs by 30–40%, improves battery utilization through professional management, extends service life via optimized charging, enables circular economy models through systematic collection and recycling, and creates stable, recurring revenue streams for manufacturers. It aligns directly with the [[battery-passport|Battery Passport]] concept, as both require comprehensive, auditable lifecycle data.

LG Energy Solution has built a comprehensive BaaS ecosystem, leveraging its manufacturing expertise to provide platform-based services. Key initiatives include:

- **KooRoo**: A battery swapping service for electric two-wheelers. Users can exchange a depleted pack for a fully charged one in about 20 seconds at dedicated stations. The system automatically inspects each battery, updating its health record and triggering redistribution for charging or, if necessary, refurbishment.
- **B-Lifecare**: Korea’s first EV battery management solution. It provides real-time range estimation, efficiency tracking, and stress monitoring via a dedicated terminal and cloud platform. Users receive proactive alerts and maintenance recommendations.
- **B·once**: A one-time battery assessment service for used EV transactions. Using LG’s big data and proprietary algorithms, it delivers an objective valuation of battery residual value, increasing transparency in the second-hand market.
- **B.around (BMTS)**: LG’s cloud- and AI-based total battery management platform covering the entire lifecycle. It aggregates data from manufacturing, in-vehicle use, second-life ESS, and recycling to optimize performance, predict end-of-life, and ensure compliance with environmental regulations.

These services position LG Energy Solution as a total solution provider in the Battery of Things (BoT) era. They also support the company’s ESG vision—"We CHARGE toward a better future"—which includes achieving 2050 carbon neutrality, 100% renewable electricity by 2030 (a full 20 years ahead of the RE100 campaign), and building a Perfect Closed Loop for battery materials by 2025. BaaS is a key pillar of this strategy, enabling resource efficiency and extending the economic life of batteries while reducing environmental impact.
