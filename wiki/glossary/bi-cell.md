# Bi-Cell

## Overview / Introduction

A bi-cell is a laminated sub‑unit used in the assembly of pouch‑type lithium‑ion batteries. It consists of five layers arranged as either **cathode / separator / anode / separator / cathode** or **anode / separator / cathode / separator / anode**. The defining feature of a bi‑cell is that its two outermost electrodes share the same polarity – a structural symmetry that distinguishes it from a [[mono-cell|Mono-Cell]], whose outer electrodes are of opposite polarity.

In LG Energy Solution’s manufacturing ecosystem, the bi‑cell serves as a semi‑finished building block. It is produced by laminating multiple electrodes and separators together, then used in the **Stack & Folding** assembly process. This approach enables dense electrode packing, improved energy density, and enhanced safety – particularly when combined with the company’s proprietary SRS (Safety Reinforced Separator) technology.

## Technical Details

### Structure and Formation

A bi‑cell is fabricated by stacking an electrode, a separator, a counter‑electrode, another separator, and finally an electrode of the same type as the first. For example, a typical positive‑outer bi‑cell has the sequence:
**cathode (⨁) → separator → anode (⊖) → separator → cathode (⨁)**.

This symmetrical arrangement means that the central electrode is of opposite polarity to the outer pair. In contrast, a mono‑cell has the outer electrodes of opposite polarity (e.g., cathode–separator–anode–separator–cathode). The bi‑cell's identical‑polarity outer layers make it especially suitable for the subsequent folding step because the folded separator film contacts only one electrode type on each side, minimizing the risk of misalignment and short circuits.

### Role in the Stack & Folding Process

The bi‑cell is the primary unit in LG Energy Solution’s **Stack & Folding** assembly method. In this process:

1. A continuous separator film is coated with a binder polymer and an active material layer – this is the **SRS (Safety Reinforced Separator)** film.
2. Pre‑fabricated bi‑cells are placed at intervals onto the moving separator film.
3. The film is folded in a Z‑shape (or “accordion” pattern), wrapping each bi‑cell between two layers of the coated separator.
4. The assembly is then heat‑pressed to bond the layers, creating a stable stack.

Because the bi‑cell already contains two internal separators (one on each side of the central electrode), and the folded SRS film adds an additional separator layer on the outside, the final stack has a **double separator barrier** between any two adjacent electrodes of opposite polarity. This redundancy greatly improves the battery’s resistance to internal shorts, thermal runaway, and mechanical deformation.

### Comparison with Mono‑Cell and AZS

| Feature | Mono‑Cell | Bi‑Cell |
|---------|-----------|---------|
| Outer electrode polarity | Opposite | Same |
| Typical assembly process | Lamination & Stacking (L&S) | Stack & Folding |
| Separator layers per unit | 2 (one on each side of central electrode) | 2 (but outer electrodes share polarity) |
| Use with SRS | Not typical | Yes – double safety layer |

LG Energy Solution’s **AZS (Advanced Z‑Stacking)** technology combines elements of both approaches. AZS uses a Z‑folded separator sheet into which individual electrodes (not pre‑laminated bi‑cells) are inserted, followed by a heat‑press lamination step. While AZS does not use bi‑cells directly, it shares the same design philosophy of maximizing electrode alignment and separator redundancy. Bi‑cells remain a core component of the company’s conventional Stack & Folding lines, particularly for high‑volume pouch cell production.

### Manufacturing Precision and Yield

The bi‑cell’s symmetrical geometry naturally reduces the risk of **overhang** – a condition where one electrode protrudes beyond the others, potentially causing internal shorts. In the Stack & Folding process, the bi‑cells are pre‑aligned before folding, and the subsequent heat‑press step (if applied) further fixes their positions. LG Energy Solution reports that such precision contributes to a manufacturing yield exceeding 99% within months of production start (as demonstrated with AZS lines in Indonesia).

## Significance / LG Energy Solution Context

Bi‑cell technology is a cornerstone of LG Energy Solution’s pouch cell manufacturing expertise. By enabling the Stack & Folding method, bi‑cells deliver three key benefits:

- **Higher Energy Density**: The dense packing of bi‑cells with alternating orientations minimizes wasted internal space, allowing more active material per unit volume.
- **Enhanced Safety**: The combination of internal bi‑cell separators and the outer SRS‑coated folding film creates a robust double‑layer safety system. This structure helps prevent internal short circuits and improves mechanical integrity during vibration or impact.
- **Process Efficiency**: Bi‑cells can be produced in large, consistent batches and then assembled into stacks with fewer handling steps compared to individual electrode stacking.

LG Energy Solution has refined bi‑cell production over two decades, integrating it with proprietary separator technologies ([[srs|SRS]]), advanced lamination chemistries, and automation. The company’s pouch cells, built using bi‑cell‑based Stack & Folding, are widely used in electric vehicles (e.g., by major global OEMs) and energy storage systems.

Looking forward, LG continues to evolve its stacking methods. While AZS represents a newer hybrid approach, bi‑cells remain relevant for applications requiring proven reliability and scale. The company’s R&D efforts focus on further improving bi‑cell alignment precision, reducing separator thickness, and increasing lamination speed – all while maintaining the safety margin that the double‑separator design provides.
