# End-of-Life Battery Reuse and Recycling

## Overview

The rapid electrification of transportation and energy storage is generating an unprecedented volume of retired lithium-ion batteries. By 2030, an estimated 90 GWh (roughly 1–2 million tons) of batteries will reach end of life each year. The global end‑of‑life battery market is projected to grow to approximately 20 trillion KRW by 2030 and 60 trillion KRW by 2050. This surge presents both a waste management challenge and a strategic resource recovery opportunity. Effective reuse and recycling are essential for reducing environmental impact, securing critical raw materials (cobalt, nickel, lithium, manganese), and building a sustainable circular economy.

## Technical Details

### Cascading End‑of‑Life Management

The battery life cycle follows a cascading strategy: **reuse** first, then **recycle**. Batteries that retain 70–80% of their original capacity are repurposed for second‑life applications, such as stationary energy storage systems (ESS), where energy density requirements are lower. After 5–10 years of second‑life service, when capacity degrades further, the batteries enter the recycling stream.

LG Energy Solution has demonstrated this approach with a commercial project at its Ochang plant in South Korea: a 100 kW ESS built from retired EV taxi batteries that had traveled over 100,000 km. The system charges during low‑cost nighttime hours and discharges during peak‑price daytime, enabling significant operational savings while proving the technical viability of second‑life batteries.

### Recycling Technologies and Material Recovery

Three primary recycling methods are used:

- **Pyrometallurgical (smelting):** High‑temperature processing recovers cobalt, nickel, and copper but loses lithium and aluminum to slag. Energy‑intensive and less efficient for lithium recovery.
- **Hydrometallurgical:** Chemical leaching (acid or base) dissolves metals, achieving recovery rates >95% for cobalt and nickel and 80–90% for lithium. This method captures a wider range of materials, including manganese.
- **Direct recycling:** The cathode crystal structure is preserved and directly reused, offering the highest process efficiency and lowest energy footprint. Requires pure, sorted feedstock.

A typical EV battery pack contains hundreds of dollars' worth of critical metals: nickel, cobalt, lithium, manganese, copper, aluminum, and graphite. LG Energy Solution's closed‑loop partnerships, such as the May 2021 agreement with North American recycler Li‑Cycle, target recovery of all these materials from production scrap and end‑of‑life batteries.

### State‑of‑Health Assessment

Accurate state‑of‑health (SoH) diagnostics are critical for determining whether a battery is suitable for reuse. LG Energy Solution continuously develops advanced battery life‑prediction techniques to precisely evaluate residual value and optimize second‑life deployment. These models integrate real‑world usage data, charge‑discharge cycle history, and degradation patterns to assess remaining useful life.

## Significance and LG Context

### Closed‑Loop Strategy

LG Energy Solution has committed to building a **resource circulation (Closed‑Loop) system** across all its global manufacturing sites by 2025. This approach covers the entire value chain: raw material procurement, production, consumption, and end‑of‑life management. In China, the company already operates a resource circulation model where post‑use batteries and production scrap are sold to recyclers; the extracted nickel and cobalt are then used to manufacture cathode materials. Similar models are being expanded in Korea, Europe, and the United States.

### Partnerships and Initiatives

- **Li‑Cycle Partnership:** In May 2021, LG's Ultium Cells joint venture with GM signed a recycling contract with Li‑Cycle, North America's largest lithium‑ion battery recycler. The partnership recovers cobalt, nickel, lithium, graphite, copper, and manganese from both production scrap and end‑of‑life batteries.
- **RE:ALL Campaign and BetteReturn Project:** LG’s first RE:ALL campaign, BetteReturn, upcycles waste separator membrane into everyday goods (cross‑body bags, tablet pouches, book covers). The project collaborates with recycling technology company Right Route, using the patented Texnic fabric—lightweight, durable, and waterproof. Sales proceeds fund ecosystem restoration. The campaign embodies the company's commitment to communicating the value of "Perfect Closed Loop" to the public.
- **Second‑Life ESS at Ochang:** As noted above, the operational ESS built from retired taxi batteries demonstrates the economic and technical feasibility of battery reuse.

### ESG Commitments

LG Energy Solution’s ESG vision, **"We CHARGE toward a better future,"** includes 8 focus areas and 4 enablers. Among the 7 core tasks is the establishment of a global recycling business model. Key targets include:
- 2050 carbon neutrality
- 2030 RE100 (100% renewable electricity) across all sites
- 2025 closed‑loop completion for all global manufacturing sites

The company's first ESG report (2021) was printed on recycled paper with soy‑based ink, reflecting its sustainability commitment. The report aligns with GRI, SASB, and TCFD standards and has been independently verified.
