# Electrode Process

## Core Idea
The electrode process makes coated cathode and anode rolls through mixing, coating, drying, calendering, and slitting/notching. It connects directly to [[dry-electrode-process|Dry Electrode Process]], [[dld|DLD (Double Layer Slot Die Coating)]], and [[pre-dispersion|Pre-Dispersion Process]].

## Technical Notes
- Electrode quality decides capacity uniformity, resistance, adhesion, porosity, and later electrolyte wetting.
- Wet electrodes dominate commercial production; dry electrodes are a strategic alternative for cost and energy reduction.
- This page links materials chemistry to manufacturing controls before assembly begins.

In the raw corpus, this concept appears as a practical engineering lever rather than a standalone textbook term. It affects how LG Energy Solution balances performance, safety, manufacturability, cost, and customer requirements.
